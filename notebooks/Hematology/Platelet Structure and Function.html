Platelet Structure and Function

Platelet Production

Platelets are produced by Megakaryoctes that reside in the bone marrow.
Fragmentation of megakaryocyte cytoplasm => platelet formation and release into bone marrow sinusoids.
Differentiation from stem cell to production of platelets takes about 10 days.

Regulation of Platelet Production

Platelet Structure

Three Zones: Peripheral, Structural, and Organelle

Peripheral Zone

Contains glycoproteins responsible for adhesion (GP1b) and aggregation (GP2a/GP3b)

  • Glycocalyx surrounds platelet, includes absorbed coagulation factors
  • Platelet membrane contains proteins involved in HLA and ABO typing

Structural Zone (Sol-Gel)

Cytoskeleton components that are responsible for the changing shape upon platelet activation

Organelle Zone

Regulates metabolism of platelet and aids in activation

  • Alpha granules: contains growth factors, hemostatic factors, angiogenic factors
    • PDGF, TGF-beta, IGF-1, Fibrinogen, Plasminogen, Factors V, VIII, XIII, VWF, VEGF and Angiostatin
  • Dense granules:
    • Contain small molecules (ATP, Ca2+, catecholamines, others)
  • Lambda granules: Lysosome-like properties

Also contains Dense Tubular System, which is site of thromboxane and prostaglandin synthesis

Platelet Function

  1. Adhesion
    a. In high shear stress settings, GP1b binds VWF, which is bound to exposed collagen in subendothelial vessel tissue
  2. Aggregation
    a. Calcium dependent GP2a/3b binds fibrinogen and VWF to cross-link and aggregate platelets

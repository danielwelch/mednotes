Normal Hematopoietic Ranges

Anemia In Children

Children are often asymptomatic until Hgb falls below 7

Diamond-Blackfan Anemia

A Pure Red Cell Aplasia caused by ribosomal protein mutations that cause apoptosis in red cell precursors

Clinical Features:

  • History of drug or toxin exposure during pregnancy
  • Low birthweight
  • Facial abnormalities
  • Upper limb abnormalities

Lab Findings:

  • Macrocytic anemia at birth
  • Increased fetal Hb and i antigen
    • Kleihauer Bettke test shows increased RBCs in peripheral blood with Hb F
  • Erythroid hypoplasia on Bone marrow

Therapy and Outcomes:

  • Prednisone - originally 3-4 times per day, then tapered until single daily dose that maintains Hb
    • Eventually, given every other day
    • 60% develop steroid dependence, and 30-40% don't respond to therapy
  • Transfusion, Prolactin during pregnancy, BM transplant

Neutropenia In Children

Schwachman-Diamond Syndrome

A syndrome characterized by exocrine pancreatic insuffiency, neutropenia, bony abnormalities

  • Caused by SBDS mutation (7q11) that encodes a ribosomal protein
  • Can lead to increased infections, aplastic anemia at a young age, and AML

Clinical Features:

  • Short stature, malnourished, and Skin rash

Treatment:

  • Oral pancreatic enzymes for pancreatic insuffiency (improves w/ age)
  • G-CSF not used, may promote transformation to AML

Thrombocytopenia In Children

TAR Syndrome

Autosomal Recessive disease causing very low thrombocytopenia that improves with time

  • Short stature and almost no forearm
  • GI/GU/Heart problems
  • Learning difficulties
  • Thumbs are present and normal (in contrast to Fanconi's)

Radioulnar Synostosis

Amegakaryocytic thrombocytopenia at brith

  • Fusion of the radius and ulna

Pancytopenia In Children

Fanconi's Anemia

Pancytopenia associated with VACTERL syndrome

  • Classic Fanconi's: Short stature, thumb abnormalities, cafe au lait spots, characteristic facial features
    • But 25% do not have physical anomalies
  • Diagnosed via chromosomal fragility testing
  • Genes involved in Fanconi's Anemia code for proteins that ubiquinate Fanc-D, which helps BRCA1 repair damaged DNA
    • Mutations cause increased risk of aplastic anemia, myelodysplasia, AML, liver tumors and other solid tumors
  • Treatment: BM transplant
